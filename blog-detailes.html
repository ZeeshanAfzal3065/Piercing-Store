What Should I Know Before Getting a Piercing?

Many opt for body piercing on the stomach, a style modification that contributes to an elegant touch of uniqueness. Whether one decides upon a regular belly button piercing or something more complex, it is hard not to succumb to the issue of what will happen when taking the plunge. PiercingStudio wants to ensure you fully understand and are comfortable with your decision. Here’s everything you need to know about belly piercing.

1. Selecting the Right Piercing Studio

Choosing a professional piercer is the first and most vital step in getting a navel piercing. Not every studio is equal; you must find an experienced expert specializing in body piercings. We here at PiercingStudio consider the utmost hygiene standards, work with sterile equipment, and are prepared to provide individual consultations for each customer. A trained piercer will guide you into the proper placement, Studs, and aftercare plan to ensure maximum healing and long-term results.

2. Piercing Process

A belly piercing is a small puncture on the belly with a sterile needle, usually followed by the insertion of a piece of stud, often a barbell. Though people say this is generally less painful, this is a short process. However, that pain might vary from one person to another since the belly is a sensitive area that exposes thin skin; even nerve endings may make it more painful than other areas. Be sure to communicate as much detail as possible with your piercer so you are comfortable during the Process.

3. Studs Choice

You would want to choose the right Stud For your belly piercing. Many people’s first stud piece tends to be a surgical steel or titanium barbell. Both of these metals are hypoallergenic and not likely to cause reactions. There are many options for ornamentation: you can have simple gems on there or get something more elaborate. Just be aware: Initial stud is often worn longer than it needs to be. It’s so, so you can wear it while swelling out during healing.

4. Recovery Phase

Like any form of body piercing, proper care must be followed to heal belly piercings correctly. In most cases, healing usually takes 6-12 months; however, it may heal faster or slower in some people. You, therefore, have a lot to do with aftercare during this period. This includes cleaning up the piercing using a saline solution, avoiding touching it with dirty hands, and avoiding swimming in pools or hot tubs, which may lead to infections.

5. Potential Contraindications and Complications

While relatively harmless, belly piercings, like other piercings, hold some risks. The primary complication associated with belly piercings is infections, likely to develop unless they are taken proper care of after getting pierced. Some other risks are stud rejection, scarring, and irritation caused by tight-fitting clothing. These risks can be significantly reduced if one uses a professional piercer, follows proper aftercare instructions, and wears loose, comfortable clothing during the healing period.

6. Pain and Aftercare

The sharpness of the pain of a belly piercing varies from person to person. Many say it’s a sharp pinch, but it is pretty short. There should be some redness, swelling, and tenderness for several days following the piercing. That may be normal, and icing helps to alleviate that. Even more important, you want to avoid touching or rotating the piercing in the healing process since that promotes bacteria and infection.

Final Thoughts

The piercing of the belly is, above all, very fashionable and funny for personal expression, but, just like with all kinds of body modification, it comes with its share of responsibilities.
